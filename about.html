Core Philosophy

Experience Science First, Learn Second

PULSE believes that the best way to understand human biology is to put your body into the feedback loop. By playing, tracking, and comparing latencies, you discover the biological limits of synapses.

1. Stimulus Reception

Whether visual, acoustic, or tactile—a stimulus triggers a receptor cell. Photons hit your retina, converting light into action potentials traveling at high velocities along the optic nerve.

2. Axonal Conduction

Action potentials travel down neuronal axons. In myelinated nerves, conduction jumps between nodes of Ranvier, allowing speeds up to 120 meters per second, versus a mere 1.5 m/s in unmyelinated fibers.

3. Motor Output

The sensory cortex communicates with the motor cortex. A decision signal is generated, traveling down the spinal cord to trigger muscle fibers, registering as a registered millisecond latency.
